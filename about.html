About the Asom Natya Sanmilon, Jorhat



The great man Sankardev started the culture of Assamese drama in Assam with his drama “Chinhayatra”. The addition of Gunaviram Baruah’s “Ramnavami” in the middle part of 1982 century, the drama movement got a momentum in Assam. In this way people continued the development of drama movement in Assam since then. With a view to develop this movement the drama organization ‘Sadou Asom Akanka Nat Sanmilan’ got birth in Dibrugrah town under the presidentship of Kalaguru Bishnu Prasad Rabha and the Sanmilon continued its activities in the field of drama. After a few years in the convention held in Mangaldoi in 1967, the constitution was amended and the organisation was renamed as “Asom Natya Sanmilan.” Since then the organisation took step to open its branches in the different parts of Assam. Many big and profound personalities like Bishnu Prasad Rabha, Prafulla Kumar Baruah, Phunu Baruah, Krishna Prasad Baruah, Lakshyadhar Choudhury, Phani Talukdar, Arun Sharma Dr. Akhil Chakraborty, Dr. Yogendra Chetia, Abdul Mazid and many more decorated the presidential chair of the Asom Natya Sanmilan acknowledged as the national organization of Assam and created the environment for further development of drama culture in Assam. Asom Natya Sanmilan had organized one act drama competition from 1959 to 1967. It started full drama (90 Minutes) competition in the year 1977 in place of one act drama. This step contributed immensely towards the creation of many renowned workers in the field of drama and many ideal and instructive drama’s. Asom Natya Sanmilan opened 14th District committees, four Mandals (zones) - Ratnapith, Kampith, Swarnapith and Soumarpith and many branches for facilitating the implementation of its agenda in the field of drama. Since its inception Asom Natya Sanmilan has been observing 12th September as “Silpi Samaroh Divas” in the memory of Natya Silpi Brajnath Sarmah. In the memory of this great personality in the field of drama the Brajnath Sharma Award has been forwarded to the person helping the development of drama culture in Assam since 2010 every year. Asom Natya Sanmilan has been organizing one act drama competition since 2005 and it has already completed its successful 8 years under the sponsorship of Oil India Ltd. Under the editorship of famous dramatist Arun Sharma a collection of selected dramas covering one and half century from 1855 to 1905 and including Gunaviram Baruah’a “Ramnavami” has already been published and the process for the publication of 2nd part is underway. The construction work of permanent office of state committee of Asom Natya Sanmilan is in progress in land area of 8 bighas and 1 katha at Birah Bebejiya in Nagaon. The branches of Asom Natya Sanmilan have been, time to time, organizing drama workshop, Recitation workshop, drama writing workshop, Janagosthiya Natya Samaroh, Child drama workshop etc. and trying their level best to give momentum to the drama in movement in Assam. Our team is Asom Natya Sanmilan, Jorhat Our group belongs to the Soumar Peeth zone. Respecting the motto of the mother organization, we are also carrying on a responsible amateur movement. The team constitutes of almost 50 members and an honorable advisor panel. Some of the dramas we have already staged are – ‘Bhobisyotor Buronji’, Yusuf – Zuleikha’ , ‘Vivah: Eti Bahir Sur’ ‘Brahmagupta’, ‘Jagatguru.com’ and ‘Dapon’ etc.’ Bhobisyotor Buronji’ received seven state level awards. Our most successful play ‘Yusuf- Zuleikha’ delighted immensely the audience of 10th Poorvottar Natya Samaroh at Sivasagar Natya Mandir, held by NSD, Delhi. The play also snatched eight state level awards till now. Another extraordinary play ‘Vivah: Eti Bahir Sur’ has been highly appreciated throughout Assam. This play also selected for 11th Poorvottar Natya Samaroh at Dimapur, held by NSD, Delhi. The play was awarded the BEST TEAM for twice and was also honoured with the SECOND and THIRD BEST TEAM for once in State Level Drama Competitions. Apart from these full length plays, the team also concentrates in one act plays, solo performances etc. Our team members namely Pankaj Kalita has achieved the BEST and Swagata Bharali has achieved the SECOND BEST solo act performer award in 7th All India Dance Drama and Folk Song competition . Our team organised a grand children workshop this summer-- from 4th July to 27th July, 2018 with around 80 participants in which the trainees boldly demonstrated three plays written by Mr. Tapos Tarun Saikia titled—‘KHEL’(Direction- Gauranga Kishor Borah), ‘LUVOR TEKELI, GHAMOR TEKELI’ (Direction- Pankaj Kalita) and ‘KOTHA KUWA GHOR’(Direction- Swagata Bharali) along with two dance-dramas. We are proud to announce that our team member Swagata Bharali got the chance to complete her theatre training from NSD, SIKKIM and Soumarjyoti Borah got chance to complete his training from NSD, Tripura. We used to run many in-house programmes to develop new methodology to boost the amateur movement, rectify the problems related to amateur theatre and to sort them out. We hope and believe that amateur theatre will help to reform the society, by offering solution to the problems of mankind.
